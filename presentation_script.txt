HOW TO SHARE THE CODE ON GITHUB
---------------------------------
Step 1 — Belyse (repo owner) creates the GitHub repo and pushes:
  git remote add origin https://github.com/YOUR_USERNAME/alu-intercampus-connect
  git push -u origin main

Step 2 — Share repo link with all team members so they can clone:
  git clone https://github.com/YOUR_USERNAME/alu-intercampus-connect

Step 3 — Each person creates their own branch and makes a small visible commit:
  git checkout -b benitha-onboarding-notifications
  (open your file, add a comment like "// Benitha - Splash & Notifications screen")
  git add lib/screens/onboarding/splash_screen.dart lib/screens/notifications/notifications_screen.dart
  git commit -m "feat: Benitha - splash screen and notifications"
  git push origin benitha-onboarding-notifications

  Repeat for each person with their branch name and files.

Step 4 — Each member opens a Pull Request from their branch into main on GitHub.
  This makes every person's contribution clearly visible.

---[ BENITHA — Splash Screen & Notifications ]---

Hey everyone, I'm Benitha and I worked on the onboarding experience
and the notifications system for our app, ALU Intercampus Connect.

So when you first open the app, you land on this splash screen. We
kept it clean and simple — it just shows the app logo and name while
everything loads in the background. First impressions matter, and we
wanted students to feel like they were opening something polished, not
just a school project.

Then we have the notifications screen. This is where students get
updates about everything happening across campuses — new events they
might like, replies to their posts, updates from communities they
follow, and reminders for events they RSVPed to.

I designed it so you can see all your alerts in one place without
having to check multiple tabs. Each notification tells you exactly
what happened and when, so nothing slips through.

That's my part — back to the team!


---[ MORDECAI — Home Feed & Explore ]---

Hi, I'm Mordecai and I handled the Home Feed and the Explore screen.

The home feed is basically the heart of the app. When a student logs
in, they see a live feed of events and posts from across all ALU
campuses. It's not just their campus — it's everything, so students
in Rwanda can see what's happening in Mauritius, and the other way
around.

Each card in the feed shows the event name, the campus it's coming
from, how many people are attending, and a quick RSVP button. We
wanted it to feel like a social feed you actually want to scroll
through, not a bulletin board.

The explore screen is for when you want to search intentionally. You
can browse by category — leadership events, hackathons, wellness,
cultural shows — and filter by campus. So if you're planning a trip
or you're curious what another campus is up to, this is where you go.

Thanks, handing it over to David.


---[ DAVID — Event Detail & My RSVPs ]---

Hey, I'm David, and I built the Event Detail screen and the My RSVPs
section.

So once you tap on an event from the feed, you land on the event
detail page. Here you get everything — a banner image, the full
description, where it's happening, the date and time, and who's
hosting it. And right there you've got the RSVP button. One tap and
you're registered.

We made sure the RSVP button is big and easy to find because that's
the whole point of the app — getting students to actually show up to
things.

The My RSVPs screen is your personal event tracker. It lists every
event you've signed up for, sorted by date, so you never forget what
you committed to. Think of it as your campus planner built right into
the app.

These two screens together make the whole event experience feel
complete — discover, read, register, and track. That's it from me,
over to Placide.


---[ PLACIDE — Chats & Chat Detail ]---

Hi everyone, I'm Placide and I worked on the communication side of
the app — the Chats screen and the Chat Detail screen.

The Chats screen is your messaging hub. It shows all your ongoing
conversations — whether that's a one-on-one message with a classmate
or a group chat for a community or event. You can see who you've
talked to recently, the last message, and whether there are unread
messages.

When you tap into a conversation, you get to the Chat Detail screen.
This is where the actual messaging happens — it's a clean chat
interface where you can send messages, see replies in real time, and
stay connected with people across campuses.

We added this because a lot of cross-campus collaboration falls apart
not because students don't want to connect, but because they don't
have a shared space to communicate. This solves that.

Alright, I'll pass it to Belyse to wrap us up.


---[ BELYSE — Communities, Create Post, Profile & Additional Feature ]---

Hi, I'm Belyse, and I worked on the communities feature, the create
post screen, the profile, and I also added an extra feature to the app.

So the Communities screen is where students can find and join groups
built around shared interests — like a debate club, a coding society,
or a cultural association. These communities aren't limited to one
campus, so you can connect with students from Mauritius, Rwanda, or
wherever ALU is.

When you tap into a community, the Community Detail screen shows you
recent posts, upcoming events tied to that group, and the members.
You can also create a new post from there using the Create Post screen
— add a title, write your content, and publish it straight to the
community.

The Profile screen shows your own page — your name, bio, campus, the
communities you've joined, and the events you're attending.

Now, for the additional feature I added — I implemented an
AI-powered event recommendation system. Based on the communities
you've joined and the events you've RSVPed to in the past, the app
learns your interests and surfaces events that are actually relevant
to you. So instead of scrolling through everything, the home feed
gets smarter the more you use it.

That's our full app — ALU Intercampus Connect. Thanks for watching!

Q2. Our app name:
------------------
ALU Intercampus Connect

This name was chosen because it directly describes what the app does:
it connects students across the African Leadership University
ecosystem — across all campuses, not just one. "Intercampus" makes
the cross-campus mission clear from the name alone.


Q3. What changes our team is planning to the screens:
------------------------------------------------------
Here are the improvements we are planning or have already implemented:

- Home Feed: We're adding a campus filter bar at the top so students
  can quickly toggle between "All Campuses", "My Campus", or a
  specific ALU campus without having to go to Explore.

- Event Detail: We moved the RSVP button to a fixed position at the
  bottom of the screen so it's always visible — you don't have to
  scroll down to find it.

- Communities: We're planning to add a "Trending" section that shows
  the most active communities this week, making it easier for new
  students to find where the energy is.

- Notifications: We're planning to add notification categories
  (Events, Communities, Messages) so students can filter what they
  see instead of seeing everything mixed together.

- Explore: We're planning to add a map view option so students can
  visually see what's happening by campus location.


Q4. Our colour palette:
------------------------
We kept ALU's core red and white as the primary brand colours, but
we extended the palette slightly:

  Primary Red  : #C0392B  (ALU signature red)
  White        : #FFFFFF  (backgrounds, cards)
  Dark Text    : #1A1A1A  (headings, body text)
  Light Grey   : #F5F5F5  (secondary backgrounds, dividers)
  Accent Red   : #E74C3C  (buttons, active states, highlights)

Sticking with ALU's colours was intentional — the app is meant to
feel like an official ALU platform, not a third-party tool.
Students should open it and immediately feel like it belongs to their
campus identity.
